Reality Check

The Litmus for “Is This Real?”

A short guide for refusing noise and choosing what improves your life now.

Three Ground Rules

1. Zero evidence = zero credibility

If a claim has no data, no mechanism, no observable effects, and no predictive power, it doesn’t earn belief. It doesn’t even earn attention.

  • No data
  • No mechanism
  • No observable effects
  • No predictive power

2. Speculative metaphysics isn’t neutral — it’s distracting

These narratives pull people out of the present, out of their agency, and out of their real life — into fear, fantasy, or dissociation.

  • Out of the present
  • Out of agency
  • Out of real life
  • Into fear, fantasy, or dissociation

Instead of helping people act, grow, or understand here, now.

3. The universe’s actual rules are few — and even those are flexible

The only “contracts” we can point to in the universe are things like gravity and the speed of light — and even those turn out to be more negotiable than we once thought.

  • Gravity
  • The speed of light

Should you spend your precious time exploring this?

  1. Does it have evidence?

    Not vibes. Not anecdotes. Not “someone said.” Actual, observable, testable grounding. If not → discard.

  2. Does it improve your life right now?

    Does it increase clarity, agency, presence, your ability to act, and your ability to not do harm? If not → it’s noise.

    • Increase clarity
    • Increase agency
    • Increase presence
    • Increase your ability to act
    • Increase your ability to not do harm

  3. Does it keep you in reality, or pull you out of it?

    A valid idea should anchor you, sharpen you, and help you operate in the world you actually inhabit. If it pulls you into abstraction, fear, or cosmic fantasy → it’s harmful.

    • Anchor you
    • Sharpen you
    • Keep you operating in real life

Keep the bar high. Reality deserves it.
